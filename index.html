// THE PROBLEM

High-mix manufacturing runs on institutional knowledge nobody has captured

Every time a new client drawing arrives, someone must read it, understand it, and recall whether the plant has done anything like it before. Right now, that someone is one overloaded senior engineer.

01

Drawing Interpretation is Slow

A junior engineer reading a GD&T-heavy drawing from a foreign client can take 40+ minutes — and still miss critical callouts. Every misread tolerance is a scrapped part or a client complaint.

02

Process Knowledge Walks Out the Door

The setup tricks, the feed rate adjustments, the tooling choices that made a difficult job work — none of it is written down. When senior engineers leave, the plant starts from zero on every similar job.

03

12–18 Month Onboarding

New hires take over a year to become useful. Onboarding means shadowing one person and hoping to absorb enough. Senior engineers spend half their time answering questions instead of doing their jobs.

04

Critical Feature Blindness

Not all tolerances are equally difficult. Seniors scan and flag the features that determine feasibility. Juniors read every dimension with equal attention — and miss the ones that actually matter.

05

Specification Cross-Referencing

A single aerospace drawing can reference 10–15 external documents — AMS, ASTM, MIL-SPEC, Boeing BPS. Each requires manual lookup, reading, and compliance verification. This invisible workload consumes hours per job.

06

Format Incompatibility

Clients send CATIA, SolidWorks, NX, STEP, IGES, PDFs — sometimes split across multiple files in different revision states. Reconciling these manually eats 15–30 minutes per job before any engineering even begins.

Interpretation and memory, in one layer

VOXXL sits between receiving a client file and beginning process planning. Upload the drawing, ask anything, and surface every relevant job your plant has ever done.

Upload a STEP file and 2D PDF. VOXXL extracts tolerances, GD&T callouts, surface finish requirements, and material specs. It flags what's difficult to machine and suggests the exact process routing required to build it — in seconds, not 40 minutes.

CORE

Natural Language Q&A

Ask the drawing anything in plain English. "What's the tightest tolerance?" "What does this GD&T symbol require?" "Which surfaces need finishing?" Answers cite the exact drawing section.

MOAT

Plant Memory — Job Archive

Every job processed builds a searchable, structured archive. Process notes, tooling choices, problems encountered, solutions found — all attached to the drawing, all retrievable by any engineer at any time.

MOAT

Similar Job Retrieval

When a new file arrives, VOXXL surfaces the most similar past jobs automatically. Same material, similar tolerances, related geometry — with process notes from those jobs immediately available.

// WHY NOW

Three forces making this problem worse every year

India's precision manufacturing sector is growing — but the knowledge infrastructure to support that growth doesn't exist yet. VOXXL is that infrastructure.

Defence indigenisation

16,000 MSMEs receiving new design files. India's defence production hit ₹1.27 lakh crore in FY24. Every indigenised component is a drawing that arrives at an Indian job shop for the first time — often in a format never seen before.

PLI scheme

New plants, zero institutional memory. PLI investments of ₹1.76 lakh crore are creating hundreds of newly certified precision manufacturers. These plants are young, have no senior engineers yet, and are immediately receiving complex high-spec client work.

China-plus-one

New global clients, unfamiliar standards. Global OEMs are actively moving precision sourcing to India. Indian job shops are winning contracts with US and European clients who send drawings to ASME or ISO standards the plant's engineers have never worked with before.

Workforce attrition

Senior engineers are retiring. The generation of engineers who built India's precision manufacturing capability is leaving. Their knowledge — accumulated over 20–30 years — has no home. VOXXL is that home.
