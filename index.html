The Problem

High-mix manufacturing runs on institutional knowledge nobody has captured

Every time a new client drawing arrives, someone must read it, understand it, and recall whether the plant has done anything like it before. Right now, that someone is one overloaded senior engineer.

PROBLEM 01

Drawing interpretation is slow and experience-dependent

A junior engineer reading a GD&T-heavy drawing from a foreign client can take 40+ minutes — and still miss critical callouts. Every misread tolerance is a scrapped part or a client complaint.

PROBLEM 02

Process knowledge walks out the door with senior engineers

The setup tricks, the feed rate adjustments, the tooling choices that made a difficult job work — none of it is written down. When that engineer leaves, the plant starts from zero on every similar job.

PROBLEM 03

New hires take 12–18 months to become useful, with no system to help them

Onboarding means shadowing one person and hoping to absorb enough. There is no system to ask, no archive to search. Senior engineers spend half their time answering questions instead of doing their jobs.

The Product

Interpretation and memory, in one layer

voxxl sits between receiving a client file and beginning process planning. Upload the drawing, ask anything, and surface every relevant job your plant has ever done.

CORE Drawing interpretation

Upload a STEP file and 2D PDF. voxxl extracts tolerances, GD&T callouts, surface finish requirements, material and heat treatment specs, and flags what's difficult to machine — in seconds, not 40 minutes.

CORE Natural language Q&A

MOAT Plant memory — job archive

Every job processed builds a searchable, structured archive. Process notes, tooling choices, problems encountered, solutions found — all attached to the drawing, all retrievable by any engineer at any time.

MOAT Similar job retrieval

When a new file arrives, voxxl surfaces the most similar past jobs automatically. Same material, similar tolerances, related geometry — with process notes from those jobs immediately available.

Why Now

Three forces making this problem worse every year

India's precision manufacturing sector is growing — but the knowledge infrastructure to support that growth doesn't exist yet. voxxl is that infrastructure.

Defence indigenisation — 16,000 MSMEs receiving new design files
India's defence production hit ₹1.27 lakh crore in FY24. Every indigenised component is a drawing that arrives at an Indian job shop for the first time — often from a foreign client, in a format never seen before.
PLI scheme — new plants, zero institutional memory
PLI investments of ₹1.76 lakh crore are creating hundreds of newly certified precision manufacturers. These plants are young, have no senior engineers yet, and are immediately receiving complex high-spec client work.
China-plus-one — new global clients, unfamiliar standards
Global OEMs are actively moving precision sourcing to India. Indian job shops are winning contracts with US and European clients who send drawings to ASME or ISO standards the plant's engineers have never worked with before.
Workforce attrition — senior engineers are retiring
The generation of engineers who built India's precision manufacturing capability is leaving. Their knowledge — accumulated over 20–30 years — has no home. voxxl is that home.
